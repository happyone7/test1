# Sports and Racing Design Reference

## Genre Promise
Deliver authentic or stylized competitive performance with strong input feel, progression loops, and fair matchmaking.

## Subgenre Split
- Sports simulation: authenticity, roster strategy, season depth.
- Sports arcade: accessible controls and spectacle pacing.
- Racing simulation: handling realism, tuning depth.
- Racing arcade: speed fantasy, drift readability, comeback mechanics.

## Core Loop
1. Enter event or match.
2. Execute control and tactical decisions.
3. Receive performance result.
4. Upgrade, train, or tune.
5. Re-enter higher-stakes events.

## Control and Feel Design
- Input response must stay consistent under pressure.
- Tune assist options to preserve accessibility while retaining mastery ceiling.
- Keep camera options aligned with readability and comfort.

## Competition Structure
- Ranked ladders with clear progression rules.
- Seasonal leagues and reset policy.
- Skill-based matchmaking with anti-smurf safeguards.

## Progression Systems
- Athlete/vehicle stat development.
- Gear or tuning depth.
- Team management and resource allocation.

## Online Fairness Rules
- Latency impact visibility and mitigation plan.
- Disconnect and abuse policy.
- Replay/telemetry for dispute analysis.

## Deliverables
- Event structure chart.
- Control tuning baseline.
- Progression and upgrade matrix.
- Matchmaking design brief.
- Season cadence and rewards plan.

## Practical Checklist
- Does input feel remain reliable across devices?
- Is progression meaningful without pay-to-win distortion?
- Are comeback mechanics exciting without invalidating skill?
