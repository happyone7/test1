# MOBA and Battle Royale Design Reference

## Genre Promise
Deliver high-clarity competitive depth where team coordination, macro decisions, and mechanical execution define outcomes.

## MOBA Section
### Core Loop
1. Draft or pick hero.
2. Secure lane economy and map pressure.
3. Contest neutral objectives.
4. Coordinate teamfights and macro rotations.
5. Convert advantage into base objective victory.

### Design Priorities
- Role identity: tank, damage, support, utility.
- Lane and jungle ecosystem balance.
- Objective timing and comeback windows.
- Hero roster diversity with counterplay clarity.

### Balance Rules
- Avoid mandatory pick/ban dominance.
- Preserve macro strategy viability beyond raw mechanics.
- Tune snowball pressure to keep comeback possibility credible.

## Battle Royale Section
### Core Loop
1. Drop decision and early loot race.
2. Mid-game rotation and resource management.
3. Zone pressure and positioning strategy.
4. Final circle execution under high information stress.

### Design Priorities
- Loot distribution fairness with controlled variance.
- Zone pacing that rewards planning and adaptation.
- Squad role synergy and revive economy tuning.
- Spectator and elimination clarity.

## Shared Competitive Integrity Rules
- Server-authoritative outcomes for combat-critical events.
- Matchmaking quality and queue health governance.
- Anti-cheat planning integrated at design stage.
- Replay and telemetry requirements for dispute handling.

## Deliverables
- Role and roster matrix.
- Objective cadence design sheet.
- Loot and zone tuning model.
- Matchmaking and rank progression policy.
- Competitive integrity risk register.

## Practical Checklist
- Are team role contributions visible and rewarding?
- Is match pacing stable across skill brackets?
- Do comeback opportunities exist without erasing skill expression?
